Note: Do not open the chamber door while conducting the CO2 OQ tests. Doing so interrupts the warm up and stabilization period for the temperature distribution study.

Note: The deviation alarms activate when the incubator detects a deviation of ±1% from the current gas set point.

Test Specification  Outcome
CO2 Low Alarm: Activation
  • Turn off the gas flow to the unit by closing the regulator.
  • Use the Up arrow to increase the CO2 set point to 7.0%.
  • A visual alarm should illuminate almost immediately after entering the new set point.
  • The audible deviation alarm should sound after 15 minutes.

Optional: You may mute the audible alarm again.

Note: During normal operations, the 15 minute delay is intended to prevent the low CO2 audible alarm from sounding every time the door is opened.

Note: The Low CO2 alarm mute functionality does not need to be tested separately from the Highalarm mute. The same system mutes the audible deviation alarm.

Turn Off or Restore CO2 Set Point

Testing of the CO2 system is complete. The CO2 system may now be set to Off, using the Down arrow button, or it may be left on. If leaving the system on, restore a flow of CO2 gas to the unit by opening the regulator valve now. Set the CO2 set point to 5%. Note that after the Temperature Distribution Study is successfully completed, the chamber door will be open for an extended period while the testing party removes the study thermocouple probes.

 

Acceptance Criteria and Results

 

Acceptance Criteria Result
CO2 High Alarms: The high deviation visual and deviation audible alarms activate and the audible alarm mutes on command.  
CO2 Low Alarms: The low deviation visual and deviation audible alarms activate and do so in accordance with the low CO2 timeline.  
No Gas Alarm: The low gas alarm activates when the flow of CO2 to the system has been interrupted as described in the test.
