Over Temperature Limit System Tests

 

OTL Tests
Test Specification Outcome
Over Temperature Limit: Activation
  • Turn the OTL knob all the way to the left (counter-clockwise) to its minimum setting.
  • The Over Temperature Activated light will illuminate.
  • The Heating Activated light should turn off.

Note: The Over Temperature Activated light may either pulse or stay on continually when the OTL is active. Both are acceptable for this test and constitute a YES answer.
Does the "Over Temperature Activated" light illuminate?
YES NO
   
Does the "Heating Activated" light turn off?
YES NO
   
Over Temperature Limit: Deactivation
  • Turn the OTL knob right, all the way to its maximum setting (clockwise).
  • The Over Temperature light should turn off.

Note: Leave the OTL control knob at its maximum position to prevent the Over Temperature Limit system from interfering with the Temperature Distribution Study. The OTL system may be set for normal operations once the Data Review procedure is complete.
Does the "Over Temperature Activated" light turn off?
YES NO
   
 

 

Acceptance Criteria and Results
Acceptance Criteria Result
The Over Temperature system activates, prevents the from heating the chamber, and deactivates as specified.  

High Limit System Tests

 

  High Limit System Tests
Test Specification Outcome
High Limit Temperature System: Activation

Set the oven High Limit setting to at least 10 degrees below the current chamber temperature and return to the oven display homepage. See the Set the High Temperature Limit procedure in the Operation chapter of the user manual.

When the High Limit cutoff activates:

  • A pair of flashing alert screens should alternate on the oven display.
  • A "4" indicator should turn on in the bottom display block.
  • The oven should cease heating.
 Does High Limit activate as described?
Yes No
   
High Limit Temperature System: Reset

Set the High Limit to 310°C, then press and hold the Reset button to clear the limit. Return to the temperature display to the homepage.

  • The alternating alert screens should cease flashing. The "4" indicator should turn off.
  • The oven should resume heating to the reach the target set point of 150°C.

Note: Leave the High Limit system set to 310°C to prevent it from interfering with the Temperature Distribution Study. The High Limit may be set for normal operations once the Data Review procedure is complete.

 Does the High Limit system turn off as described?
Yes No
   
 

 

Acceptance Criteria and Results
Acceptance Criteria Results
The High Limit System activates, prevents the oven from heating the chamber, and deactivates as specified.
