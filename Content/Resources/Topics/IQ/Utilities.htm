Visual Inspection and Testing
  Utilities: Power and Gas and Vacuum Supply
Verification Specification Outcome Notes
Power: Voltage. Record the voltage of the power supply system. The supply voltage must not vary more than ±5% from the rating on the Data Plate.    
Power: Record the amperage of the power supply system. The supplied amperage equals or is greater than the amperage on the unit data plate.    
Power: Verify the has been hardwired (permanent connect wiring) to a wall power source. Visually verify the is connected to the wall power source.    
Power: Is the wall power source circuit-breaker sufficient to safeguard the , yes or no? See the Power Source Requirements entry in the Installation chapter of the user manual for the required circuit-breaker amperage.    
Air Supply: Is a compressed air supply on hand sufficient to operate the vacuum system valves? The air supply can deliver 70 – 80 psi at the air fitting for as long as the is on and operating.    
Gas Plumbing: The cylinder or building CO2 regulator meets requirements, yes or no? A dual-stage CO2 regulator is installed and meets the requirements specified in the Installation chapter of the user manual.    
Gas Plumbing: The CO2 supply is correctly plumbed to the unit, yes or no? Gas tubing is securely connected to the supply system regulator and the unit CO2 inlet port.    
Gas Plumbing: N2, yes, no, or N/A. A dual-stage nitrogen regulator is installed and meets the requirements specified in the Installation chapter of the user manual.    
Gas Plumbing: An N2 supply is correctly plumbed to the unit, yes, no, or N/A? Gas tubing is securely connected to the supply system regulator and the unit inlet port.    

 

 

 

Acceptance Criteria and Results
Acceptance Criteria Result
Power: The power supply meets the manufacturer voltage, circuit breaker, and amperage specifications.  
Air Supply: The compressed air supply delivers sufficient pressure to actuate the vacuum system valves while the is in operation.  
Gas Plumbing: The supplemental N2 gas supply has a proper regulator and is securely plumbed to the unit, capable of delivering a steady gas flow of 50 psi.  
Gas Plumbing: the CO2 supply has a proper regulator and is securely plumbed to the unit, capable of delivering a steady gas flow of 15 – 20 psi.
